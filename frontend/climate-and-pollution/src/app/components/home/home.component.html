Air Quality Index levels

0-50: Good – Air quality is considered satisfactory, and air pollution poses little or no risk.

51-100: Moderate – Air quality is acceptable; some pollutants may slightly affect sensitive individuals.

101-150: Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups – Sensitive groups may experience health effects.

151-200: Unhealthy – Everyone may experience health effects; sensitive groups may experience more serious effects.

201-300: Very Unhealthy – Health alert: everyone may experience serious health effects.

301-500: Hazardous – Health warnings of emergency conditions; the entire population is likely to be affected.

Long-term exposure to poor air quality, like in Beijing, can lead to several serious health issues:

  • Respiratory diseases
  • Cardiovascular problems
  • Cancer
  • Weakened immune system
